Course Modules

Introduction to Graphic Design Tools

Module Overview:

This module aims to provide an introduction to two popular graphic design tools, Canva and Photoshop. Students will gain hands-on experience with these tools and develop foundational skills in graphic design.

Week 1: Getting Started with Canva

Session 1: Introduction to Canva

- Overview of Canva's interface and features.

- Creating a Canva account and setting up profiles.

Session 2: Basic Design Elements in Canva

- Understanding design principles in Canva.

- Creating simple designs (social media posts, flyers).

Session 3: Advanced Features in Canva

- Exploring Canva's advanced features.

- Designing more complex projects (infographics, presentations).

Week 2: Photoshop Basics

Session 4: Introduction to Photoshop

- Overview of Photoshop's workspace and tools.

- Fundamentals of image editing.

Session 5: Working with Layers and Masks

- Understanding layers and their significance.

- Basic techniques for using masks.

Session 6: Photo Retouching and Manipulation

- Techniques for enhancing and retouching photographs.

- Creative manipulation of images.

Week 3: Integrating figma and Photoshop

Session 7: Exporting and Importing

- Exporting designs from figma for use in Photoshop.

- Importing figma elements into Photoshop projects.

Session 8: Collaboration and Workflow

- Collaborative design workflows between Canva and Photoshop.

- Best practices for seamless integration.

Week 4: Project and Portfolio Development

Session 9: Design Project

- Applying skills learned in a practical project.

- Feedback and critique sessions.

Session 10: Portfolio Building

- Organizing and presenting design projects in a portfolio.

- Tips for creating an impressive design portfolio.

Who Is This Course For Graphic Design Course

1. Beginners in Graphic Design:

- Individuals who are new to graphic design and want to learn the basics.

- Those with little to no experience in using graphic design tools.

2. Aspiring Designers:

- Individuals who aspire to become graphic designers or work in related creative fields.

- Students or professionals exploring a career change into graphic design.

3. Marketing and Communication Professionals:

- Marketing professionals who want to enhance their design skills for creating effective promotional materials.

- Communication specialists interested in improving visual communication through graphic design.

4. Small Business Owners and Entrepreneurs:

- Small business owners who need to create their own marketing materials.

- Entrepreneurs looking to understand the basics of graphic design for branding purposes.

5. Freelancers and Content Creators:

- Freelancers who want to offer graphic design services.

- Content creators (bloggers, YouTubers, etc.) who wish to enhance their visual content.

6. Anyone Interested in DIY Design:

- Individuals who want to create their own graphics for personal projects, social media, or events.

It's important to note that the course's level (introductory, intermediate, advanced) and specific content may influence the suitability for certain audiences. Beginners would benefit the most from an introductory course, while more advanced designers might prefer courses with a focus on advanced techniques and industry trends.

Always check the course description and prerequisites to ensure that it aligns with your current skill level and learning goals.
